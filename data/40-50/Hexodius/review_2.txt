Hexodius is an arena twin stick shooter.  This means you have a character /ship/geometric shape/whatever, in a square area where there are enemies.  You shoot in any direction to deal with them and they shoot back.  Rarely is there any variation in terrain in these types of games.  Hexodius is no exception.  A few levels are longer than the screen and some have some indestructible boxes.  But, thus far I have seen nothing majorly abnormal for a TSS.  It is definitely best to have a twin stick controller for this particular game.  

There is a sort of overworld area sort of like the overworld map in Super Mario World.  But it's not a huge difference.  You can only visit levels once and thereâ€™s no real choices to make. And that is the crux of why I'm not recommending this, even after updating the review.  It's prettier.  And it has some bells and a few whistles.  But, overall, you can find as good as or better for free on Kongregate.  The only place Hexodius shines over a free Unity or Flash equivalent is the artwork, which is very nice for a game of this genre.  So, final word, so to speak, this isn't horrible, or even particularly bad, but you can get better cheaper or free.  Wait for a sale if you must have this.
